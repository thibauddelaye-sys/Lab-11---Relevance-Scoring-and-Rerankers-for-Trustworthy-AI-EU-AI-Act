# Lab Summary — Relevance Scoring & Rerankers

In our 7-query evaluation comparing Baseline / +Filter / +Rerank pipelines on EU AI policy and Trustworthy AI podcast content, reranking with `ms-marco-MiniLM-L-6-v2` was clearly best on the podcast queries (Q5–Q7): on Q5 ("AI trust") it surfaced the "AI worthy of trust + Ethics Guidelines" framing where Baseline and Filter only retrieved the generic black-box reliance point, and on Q6 it found surveillance/tracking concerns the other pipelines missed entirely. However, reranking **regressed** on 2 of 4 regulation queries — Q2 (high-risk requirements) and Q3 (transparency definition) both returned "I cannot find this information" under Rerank while Baseline answered correctly, raising the unhelpful-answer rate from 0/7 (Baseline) to 2/7 (+Rerank). To test whether this was a model-size issue or a deeper problem, we re-ran Q2 and Q3 with `ms-marco-electra-base` (~400 MB vs ~80 MB): ELECTRA failed identically — for Q3 both models returned the same English podcast chunk about location data instead of the regulation's definition, and for Q2 both retrieved unrelated French regulation chunks. This revealed the true cause: the source document is in French, but both cross-encoders are trained exclusively on English MS MARCO data and cannot reliably score French text. By contrast, OpenAI's `text-embedding-3-small` (used for vector search) is multilingual, which is why Baseline and Filter pipelines worked correctly. **Recommendation:** reranking helps on same-language noisy sources like the English podcast, but applying English-only cross-encoders to multilingual document corpora causes systematic regression; the fix is either a multilingual cross-encoder (e.g. `mmarco-mMiniLMv2`) or translating documents at ingest time. In production, always match the reranker's training language to your corpus before enabling it.
